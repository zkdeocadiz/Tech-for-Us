# How do I write a better dating profile?
Disclaimer: The aim of this is not to get ***more*** matches, but to get ***better*** matches from dating apps

## What’s the aim of a dating profile?
You’re showing ***yourself*** off to ***possible partners***. 

There’s two components of that: yourself and possible partners. However, when writing our dating profile, we usually only focus on the first while neglecting to fully think about the second. The problem is that the second is actually the more important of the two because a dating profile is a marketing exercise.

With marketing, there are three main things to consider: the target audience, the product (that’s you!), and the platform. We usually have to think about it in that order, because the product needs to have people who want to receive it in order to be considered successful and needs to be made with their needs in mind so the product will be appealing in the end.

## The target audience
It’s important to know the type of person you’re looking for. Most people think “oh, that’s easy!” and immediately say “[a man in finance, with a trust fund, 6' 5", blue eyes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Looking_for_a_Man_in_Finance)”. But that’s not really the end of it. The more important thing is to know why you want those traits in someone.

If you want someone who loves to attend events and commits to the bit... do you want that because your interests and teh events tied to them are a core part of your identity and you want to feel supported in what you love? Or are you looking for someone who doesn’t take things too seriously because you’re in a stage of your life where having fun is the most important thing to you?

If you want a man in finance, with a trust fund, 6’5”, blue eyes, is it because you’re looking for financial freedom because debt is a huge concern for you? Or are you looking not to work and want a provider?

The difference in your motivation for wanting those characteristics is important so you know what you really care about in a partner.

### What do you know about this person?

So all of those things that you value also signal something about you. You have assumptions around how this person lives their life, which is great to have but may lead you to match with people who might not actually live up to you hopes and dreams. Let’s list some of those assumptions!

Some examples:
- if you love someone who attends lots of events, you might see them as having a packed schedule full of events they’re going to. You might see them as always on the hunt for cool events to tell their friends about because of that.
- if you’re looking for a friend with benefits who has a high libido, they might be prioritizing convenience and may not be looking to rearrange their schedule for you

Why is this important? It’s good to know the things that really draw you in about their life. Make those things explicit for yourself so you know what you’re looking for.

## The Product (you!)
Now that we know our target audience a little better, it’s time to start thinking about how you, as the product, relate to that target audience. Where do you come into the picture? What can you offer this person?

For someone who has a packed schedule and is always looking for events to go to with friends, it could be as simple as you being on many local event venues’ newsletters, so you can tell them about cool things coming up.

If they’re successful and personally driven, maybe your personality is that of a cheerleader and you find little ways to celebrate both small and big wins of the people that you care about.

Now, this is isn’t the place to start making things up. For there to be a mutual connection, there should be things that appeal to them in your life already. Not having any way to contribute to their life means significantly more chance of things not working out due to conflicts in what want to do together. If you have nothing to add to their life that you also enjoy, you should think of things that would be easy to integrate into your life... and then actually do them.

### Making Yourself Distinctive
Now a lot of what people want in a partner is fairly common—we all want to feel valued and respected, and there are some common ways that we’ve learned make us feel that way. But if many people can meet that criteria, what would make this person want to pick you?

While dating apps may flatten this a bit, since many folks are swiping right on a ton of people at once, it’s also important to have something that can help people start a conversation that actually gets towards the things you really like.

How do you write a profile that helps people both pick you and talk to you about something interesting? The answer is making yourself more distinctive. What is something that only you can say?

It’s one thing to say you’re into crafting, and another to say that you’re such a prolific knitter that you’ve knitted every present you’ve given in the past eight years. Someone who loves live music may get a kick out of hearing that you twerked in the circle pit and had to go to physical therapy afterwards—and it’s something that they’d definitely remember you by when you eventually match.

## The platform
Now you have a ton of stuff that can populate a profile that will hopefully draw in someone that you want to meet! But how do you actually put it into your profile?

This obviously depends on your dating app of choice. Some apps have prompts to answer within a set character count, while others are more freeform. Either way, you can fit your details into that format—just take your time to choose your best options from what the dating app gives you to work with.

You can also think about choosing the dating app that has a format that aligns best with the way that you communicate. If you write a lot (like I do!), you can think about choosing an app that lets you write at length (OkCupid, Feeld). If you don’t enjoy a lot of text back-and-forth, choose an app that has a much smaller word count (Tinder, Bumble, Hinge).

### Picking Photos

This is a key part of dating profiles, because many folks often don’t read profiles. Your photos should tell the same story as the things you’ve listed.

Someone who has a bunch of photos of themself grinning in various silly costumes surrounded by others dressed similarly would be read very differently from someone who has a bunch of photos of themselves in ropes and chains.

Some best practices:
- You should aim to show your face—people respond better to faces, which is why we tend to find faces in inanimate objects all the time.
- Make an effort. Don’t just have a photo of you reflected in your dirty bathroom mirror. Clean up, wear something nice, and maybe ask someone else to take the photo.
- Have some variety. The photos should tell a story and have different things that might draw different people in. More variety means more things for people to possibly latch onto
- Make sure you have a reason for the photo you’ve chosen. Don’t just add more photos to fill up the space. More options mean more things for people to like... but also more chances to find something that will turn them off.

### Matching With People
You’ve written your profile, now you’re swiping. But if your aim is to actually get better matches, it can’t just stop at your profile. You have to make some judgements about people that will dictate what your experience is like.

Good thing you have that list of things you value in someone from earlier!

People may not be the best at writing their profiles, but you should be able to find a hint towards any of those values for you to swipe right on someone. This might mean you lose out on a potential match, but unless you’re matching with people to generate terrible stories to tell your friends... you’re very likely to have a terrible time with them anyway.

### Writing an Intro Message

Congrats—you’ve matched with someone that’s likely to vibe with you! Now it’s time to actually open up the conversation.

No one likes a “wyd?” message, but it’s super common to receive one. What do you even reply to that if you’re looking for a productive conversation?

Think about something like the following: I love that ***you host regular game nights*** with friends! What types of games do you enjoy playing at them? ***I’m a big co-op game fan*** because ***I’m admittedly not really competitive***.

This response is made up of 3 parts:
1. ***Compliment*** them on something that you found in their profile. Everyone loves being complimented and it shows that you’ve paid attention to them specifically.
2. ***Ask a question*** about what you mentioned. It shows your curiosity about something specific about them, and something that they can easily answer because it’s already familiar to them.
3. ***Relate it*** to your own experience. This shows that you can fit into their life easily and gives them something to bounce off of and relate to in return.

The aim is to show you’ve paid attention and that you genuinely are interested in them. People tend to respond in a similar way to what you approached them with, so if that’s the type of response you’re looking for, show them how to do it first.

### Identify Other Avenues

Let’s be honest. You can do everything right, have the best profile for what you’re looking for, and still strike out. The dating apps really aren’t designed to help you succeed (how else would they make money?).

If you find yourself getting frustrated and constantly thinking about how dating apps are not working for you, take a break for a few weeks. You’ll likely get rewarded when the apps try to entice you back—they’ll start giving you more matches or perks.

If you don’t want to put finding a partner on pause for that time, identify other ways you might be able to encounter people that match what you’re looking for.

### Don’t Stress

It’s always okay to remember that your success or failures on dating apps doesn’t define anything about you as a person or your value as a potential partner.

Dating sucks, even without dating apps. And even when it seems to be a good experience, things don’t always work out in the way you wanted them to. However, if you lead with your values and show up in ways that align with them, you’ll hopefully have a much better experience than you’ve had before.

There will always be people and dating apps out there that suck and give you a terrible experience. But if we push forward in centering our own personal values, the failures of dating apps and the random people we meet don’t have to define our entire dating experience.

I hope you have an amazing time finding the people that you want to introduce into your life.

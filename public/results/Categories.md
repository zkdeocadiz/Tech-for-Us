# Categories

## How You Feel
### Energized (E) vs Weary (W)
Being **Energized** means that you gain energy from being on social tech, which can act as a positive driver for you to use it. You get a kick out of being able to talk to people through the digital space. You might find it easier to communicate digitally than in person, or you might just enjoy spending time with the folks you meet online a lot more than the people you can meet in person. However, it might be worth remembering that while digital spaces can energize you, things are probably not so black and white. You might *also* be able to get some of those benefits in the analog world, and you might find that digital spaces aren't the best fit for everyone you meet.

Being **Weary** means that you find yourself feeling drained from being on social tech platforms. It actively takes a lot of effort to use them and you are likely to feel dissatisfied with the role these platforms play in your life. However, it might be important to identify what *exactly* makes you weary of using social tech, because those stressors might hide under the surface of some of your in-person relationships as well, especially as social tech and the platform norms become more embedded in the ways that we communicate with the people we care about.

## The Role Social Tech PLays
### Desired (D) vs Mandatory (M)
Being **Desired** means that you see yourself aligned with the typical benefits that social tech can give you, and the ways the platforms keep you engaged fit with what feels rewarding to you. You feel like those platforms give you the right types of incentives. However, it can be easy to feel like your experience is universal and not question how the incentives of social tech aren't always aligned with what's best for other people.

Being **Mandatory** means that you find yourself feeling obligated to be on social tech platforms. Whether that's because you feel like you'll lose out on great opportunities or because feel like you won't connect as deeply with people without spending your time in a shared digital space, you likely have frustrations around how much of social tech runs your life. But it's important to remember that things aren't so black and white, and there can be ways to set your boundaries with social tech that still help you connect with the people you want to.

## How You Show Up
### Genuine (G) vs Filtered (F)

Being **Genuine (G)** means you feel like you truly show up as yourself in digital social spaces. You don't feel the pressure to change the way you present yourself online, but that also means you might overlook the impact of not filtering yourself in digital public spaces. When we don't code switch based on where we are and who we're talking to, both in digital spaces and physical ones, we run the risk of getting in our own way and not adapting to different social situations. Be careful you're not using being genuine to yourself as a stand in for being an asshole.

Being **Filtered (F)** means that you feel like you filter yourself, your identity, and/or your thoughts when connecting with others on social tech.

#### Polished (Fp) vs Tracked (Ft)

**Filtered (F)**  can be split into two categories:
- Being **Polished (Fp)** means that you filter yourself mostly because you want to portray a specific version of yourself, often to show yourself off in the best light. This isn't necessarily a bad thing—we all do some form of this filtering, even without technology in the mix—but it can get in the way of showing up in a way that gets you to more deeply connect with someone else. If you are constantly filtering yourself so you look good online, you might lack digital spaces where you feel comfortable with people looking past your polished veneer to see and recognize the complexity that makes you who you are.
- Being **Tracked (Ft)** means that you filter yourself mostly because you are fearful about the effects of being tracked by the platforms or by other people, where things you say or do may come back to haunt you. It's good to be aware of this—what happens on the internet stays on the internet forever, for everyone to see—but the fear means you also miss out on all the experiences that might really just be inconsequential.

## How You Relate to Others
### Connected (C) vs Lonely (L)
Being **Connected (C)** means that you feel like you have genuine connections with people online.
It's worth reflecting on your digital relationships, though, and whether or not those connections are good for you—much like we often need to do with our in-person relationships. If the platform you use most with them disappeared tomorrow, would you still maintain those connections?

Being **Lonely (L)** means that you struggle to feel connected to people through social tech. You might have all the aspirations of wanting to connect with people, but the digital spaces you use really just leave you feeling more alone. It's worth asking what you want to do in this moment—abstain from social tech due to the stressors you've been encountering on the platforms you use, or find ways to adapt, misuse, or break the platforms that you use to make it more likely you find meaningful connections online.

# EMFL — The Doom Scroller
*energized, mandatory, filtered (polished), lonely*

Social tech holds your attention and keeps you coming back — but it's not delivering real connection or genuine self-expression, and you're not sure why you're there beyond a vague sense of knowing you *should* be on there. Because of that feeling, you find yourself curating yourself online and only showing off what you think represents you in the best light... although you might struggle with actually posting about your life. Instead, you likely see yourself doom scrolling and just consuming content, trying to find a connection with others, but the only thing you receive the dopamine hit of continuous content in return for all of your engagement.

It may be worth asking yourself what you're actually getting out of using social tech. What are your goals by using it and how can it best serve *you*?

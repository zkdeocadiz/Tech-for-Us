# EMFL — The Doom Scroller
*energized, mandatory, filtered (tracked), lonely*

Social tech holds your attention and keeps you coming back — but it's not delivering real connection or genuine self-expression, and you're not sure why you're there beyond a vague sense of knowing you *should* be on there. However, even as it feels like you *need* to be on social platforms, you also know how dangerous it can be to share your true thoughts publicly online. So you instead are more likely to see yourself doom scrolling and just consuming content, trying to find a connection with others, but the only thing you receive the dopamine hit of continuous content in return for all of your engagement.

It may be worth asking yourself what you're actually getting out of using social tech. What are your goals by using it and how can it best serve *you*?
